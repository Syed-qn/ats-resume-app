Understanding ATS: The Hidden Job Market Filter

Learn why 98% of Fortune 500 companies use Applicant Tracking Systems and how they impact your job search success

What is an ATS?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software that automatically scans, parses, and ranks resumes before any human recruiter sees them. It looks for specific keywords, formatting, and job-relevant information.

of Fortune 500 companies use ATS

The Filtering Process

ATS systems automatically reject 75% of resumes within seconds based on keyword matching, formatting issues, and missing requirements. Only the top-ranking resumes reach human recruiters.

of resumes never reach human eyes

Keyword Scanning

ATS software scans for exact keyword matches from the job description. Missing key terms means automatic rejection, even if you're perfectly qualified for the position.

6 sec
average time ATS spends on each resume

Format Requirements

ATS systems struggle with creative layouts, graphics, tables, and non-standard fonts. Clean, simple formatting with standard headings ensures proper parsing and higher compatibility scores.

of resumes fail due to formatting issues
